Section 1

Executive Summary

Lawei Homestay is a new (<1 year), 4-room / 13-guest owner-run homestay in Mawlai, Shillong, operating today almost entirely on paper registers, notebooks, WhatsApp, phone calls and verbal coordination. The owner's single most important goal is unambiguous: stop losing bookings to slow and after-hours replies (he estimates 6–15 lost enquiries a month), followed by less manual coordination, after-hours handling, scam/bad-guest defence, and keeping all records in one place.

Two products are being assessed together. The OTA Ops Hub is the deterministic system of record — a genuinely capable Next.js PMS. ROOT is a separate agentic AI platform (multilingual chat, booking/OTP automation, driver dispatch, plain-language console, human-in-the-loop safety) that transacts through the OTA's guarded API. This split is deliberate and sound: the database is truth, and the AI never takes sensitive actions directly.

The headline finding is that the prototype is materially more complete than a first-look would suggest. The correctness core — derived availability and a database-level no-double-booking guarantee — is excellent, and the operational spine (bookings, unified calendar, today dashboard, guest CRM, check-in/out, advisory pricing, finance with true net profit, analytics, invoices, CSV export, iCal, an OTA-email inbox, escalations/HITL, an agent API seam, a scam-number list, advance-payment tracking and a messaging outbox) is built or scaffolded. Roughly 55% of the discovery requirements are met today.

The gaps cluster into three bands. (1) The #1 pain is not yet closed inside software: the messaging outbox only logs — nothing is actually sent — and the conversational agents that would answer guests after hours are the not-yet-deployed ROOT layer. Until a WhatsApp Business provider is wired and the ROOT Assistant is live, the owner's top problem remains. (2) Whole operational modules are absent: complaints, maintenance/assets, inventory, staff & role-based access, vendors/procurement, transport records, tours, reviews, and a proper refund/cancellation-policy engine. (3) The client's ambitious "Rapido for homestays" community vision — overflow referrals with credit tracking, shared availability, a searchable property directory, shared scam and bad-guest networks, and no-show reliability scoring — is essentially unbuilt, because it depends on multi-tenancy which is intentionally deferred.

Bottom line: The backbone is real and the architecture is right. To make Lawei a credible daily-use deployment, Phase 1 must (a) turn on live messaging + the ROOT Assistant, (b) add a configurable cancellation/refund engine and a complaints module, and (c) fill in the guest/ID/preference fields recorded on paper today. The community network is a compelling but later epic gated on multi-tenancy.
Section 2

Business Understanding

Business model & property type

Owner-operated homestay — 4 rooms, max 13 guests, opened within the last year, in Mawlai Umshing, Shillong. ADR ₹2,100–3,500; average occupancy 50–70%; "most" business is repeat or referred. Amenities: meals/kitchen, Wi-Fi, parking, bonfire/camping, airport/station pickup, family rooms. The owner also accommodates drivers. Languages in use: Khasi, English, Hindi. Seasonality: Oct–Dec peak, Jan–Apr lean, May–Jul peak.

Operational workflow (today)

The owner personally handles enquiries, bookings, payments, check-in, complaints and referrals. Six people help across housekeeping, cooking, reception and deliveries/pickups, coordinated verbally. There is no software; the "system" is a paper register, a paper availability chart, WhatsApp and memory.

Guest journey

Enquiry (phone/WhatsApp/Insta/walk-in) → informal quote → advance ₹500+ to hold → verbal/WhatsApp confirmation → arrival & ID capture on paper → stay (owner fields issues: geyser, food, slow Wi-Fi, timing) → checkout → receipt on request.

Booking journey

Sources: phone, WhatsApp, walk-in, own website, Instagram/Facebook, travel agents, word of mouth. Planning to list on MakeMyTrip, Goibibo, Agoda, Cleartrip. Availability checked against a paper chart; confirmations are verbal or WhatsApp.

Revenue workflow

Cash and UPI/GPay/PhonePe; advance "always" taken. Payments and pending amounts tracked on paper; receipts on request; not GST-registered; books kept in a notebook; wants exportable data for an accountant.

Staff workflow

6 staff (housekeeping, cook, reception, delivery/pickup). Tasks and shifts assigned verbally. Money/revenue should be visible "only for owners" — an explicit role-based access requirement.

Communication channels

WhatsApp (primary), phone, in-person, Instagram DM. 30–60 guest messages on a busy day. Replies "within minutes" during the day but "late" after hours — the crux of the lost-business problem. Language differences do cause friction.

Pain points (owner's own ranking)

  1. Losing bookings to slow / after-hours replies (ranked #1; 6–15 lost enquiries/month).
  2. Too much manual coordination.
  3. No after-hours handling.
  4. Scams (was a fake-payment victim) and bad guests.
  5. Records scattered — wants one place.

Future growth vision

"Start small, grow" to multiple locations, and a "Rapido type of application for guesthouses/homestays" — a real-time regional network for overflow referrals, shared availability, shared scam/bad-guest alerts and shared transport. This is the north star and shapes several architectural recommendations below.

Section 4

Future Business Process ("to-be")

The target operating model keeps the owner in control but removes manual bottlenecks by splitting responsibilities cleanly between the two systems:

  • OTA Ops Hub = system of record. Every booking, guest, payment, block and escalation lives here. Availability is always derived; double-booking is impossible at the database level.
  • ROOT = always-on front door. The multilingual Assistant answers enquiries and FAQs in Khasi/Hindi/English 24/7, holds and confirms simple bookings through the guarded API, sends reminders and payment requests, and files anything sensitive (cancellations, refunds, suspected scams) to the human-in-the-loop escalation queue.
  • Staff get scoped access. Reception sees bookings but not finances; housekeeping sees room turnover; the owner sees everything.
  • The community layer (later) turns "share a contact in a WhatsApp group" into tracked overflow referrals with credit balances, shared availability and verified scam/bad-guest alerts.
The desired future flow: Guest messages ROOT (any language, any hour) → ROOT quotes & holds via the OTA API → OTA records the booking with the double-booking guarantee → ROOT confirms and later reminds → sensitive actions escalate to the owner → analytics and money stay in the OTA, owned locally.

Section 7

Complete Gap Analysis

The 27 distinct gaps, consolidated. The full register (with owning system, status, priority and phase) is the "Gap Register" sheet of the companion workbook; this is the narrative view grouped by theme.

Theme A — The #1 pain is not yet closed in software

  • Live guest messaging — the outbox only logs; WhatsApp/SMS/email are not actually sent. WhatsApp is rated "essential" (5/5). Critical
  • Conversational AI agents — the Assistant/FAQ/booking agents that answer after hours are the ROOT layer, not yet deployed against the ready seam. Critical

Theme B — Absent operational modules

  • Complaints & guest issues — no logging, categories, priority, assignment, resolution or CSAT. Owner wants to track patterns. High
  • Maintenance & assets — only room blocks exist; no requests, history, costs, photos, asset register or preventive maintenance. High
  • Inventory & supplies — none; owner tracks "in my head" and runs out sometimes. Medium
  • Staff & role-based access — single-owner only; "money only for owners" is an explicit RBAC requirement. High
  • Vendors & procurement — no directory, POs, goods-received, payments or ratings. Medium
  • Transport records — no driver/vehicle/trip records in OTA (ROOT CabAgent handles live dispatch). High
  • Tours & activities — no packages, partners, guides or commission. Low
  • Reviews & reputation — no solicitation, tracking or response workflow. Medium

Theme C — Booking, money & compliance depth

  • Refunds & cancellation policy — the 4-day / peak-2-day windows are not encoded or configurable; no refund entity or partial refunds. High
  • Group & long-stay bookings — single-room bookings only, though the owner rents the whole property. Medium
  • Guest fields — address, vehicle number and preferences captured on paper have no DB field. High
  • ID verification & upload — text ID only; upload not activated; no verified/checked flags or retention policy. High
  • Online payment collection — no UPI/Razorpay link. High
  • Audit log & consent — privacy is the owner's stated concern, but there is no audit trail or consent capture. Medium

Theme D — The regional community network ("Rapido for homestays")

  • Multi-tenancy & real auth — the gating epic for both multi-location growth and every community feature. High
  • Overflow referral network — no tracking of recipient, conversion, revenue, favour or credit balance. The client's headline idea. High
  • Shared availability & searchable directory — no cross-property visibility or filtering (pets, wheelchair, big groups, parking, pickup). Medium
  • Community scam & bad-guest network — single-property lists exist; no export, verified sharing, evidence or appeal workflow. High
  • No-show reliability scoring — status exists; no score or shared repeat-offender flag. Medium
  • Amenities model — no facilities data, which blocks listings and the directory. Medium

Section 18

Technical Recommendations

  • Protect the correctness core. Never weaken the GiST no-double-booking constraint or the derived-availability rule; route every new booking path (including agents) through the shared create logic. Keep the safe db:migrate:new discipline for the generated DATERANGE columns.
  • Make multi-tenancy the pivotal epic. The schema already stubs nullable property_id and agent contracts accept propertyRef; do the tenancy/auth work deliberately as one piece — it unlocks growth and the entire community network.
  • Provider-agnostic messaging. Keep the LogAdapter seam; add WhatsApp/SMS adapters behind it so callers don't change. Treat the BSP as a flagged, cost-bearing dependency.
  • Money precision. Today money is whole-rupee number; move to integer-paise or decimal before serious accounting/GST.
  • Object storage for ID/maintenance photos (Supabase Storage adapter exists — activate the bucket + env).
  • Test breadth. Add route-level create-overlap→409 and housekeeping-derivation tests; the pure pricing/conflict/availability logic is already covered.
  • Audit & consent tables for privacy compliance (the owner's stated concern) and to support "your data stays yours".
  • Offline-tolerant PWA patterns for patchy connectivity.

Section 22

Final Recommendations

  1. Ship the front door before anything else. The client's business case, his grant pitch and his ranked #1 pain all point to one thing: an always-on multilingual agent that answers and books after hours. That means deploying the ROOT Assistant and wiring a WhatsApp provider into the existing outbox. Everything else is secondary until this is live.
  2. Bank the strong foundation. The derived-availability + database-enforced no-double-booking core is genuinely excellent and rare in small-property software. Protect it, and lead the demo with it.
  3. Close the honest, unglamorous gaps. A configurable cancellation/refund engine, a complaints log, and the guest/ID/preference fields that are on paper today are what make this a real daily tool rather than a demo — and they are all modest, well-scoped builds.
  4. Treat multi-tenancy as the hinge. It gates both the owner's "multiple locations" ambition and the entire "Rapido for homestays" community network. Plan it as one deliberate epic, not a refactor squeezed in later.
  5. Sequence the community vision, don't skip it. Overflow referrals with credit tracking, shared availability and verified scam/bad-guest networks are the differentiator the client is most excited about — but they carry real fairness/defamation risk and depend on tenancy. Build them third, with evidence and appeal safeguards designed in.
  6. Keep the OTA/ROOT split clean. The database is truth; the AI transacts through guarded APIs and escalates sensitive actions to a human. This is the right architecture — the whole roadmap should preserve it.
